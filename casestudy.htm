CASE STUDY / EXAMPLES

The original proposal for this simple web app was to show how one of the roles of a media producer could be almost fully automated through the use of large language models, specifically OpenAI's Chat GPT. For example, at a major network news organization in the mid-2010s, it was typical to employ producers who would watch news wires, broadcasts, and Twitter feeds in order to quickly and accurately produce the following:

  1. text-based articles for publishing to one or more news-branded or lifestyle websites,
  2. review and optimize articles for best SEO performance,
  3. create a series of social media posts for promotion of said web articles via Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn,
  4. create a television broadcast script for quick production of a web-exclusive video segment,
  5. forward a summary recap to editors at various syndication partners and topical-interest sites, and,
  6. log a note off the published posts in a daily rundown.
Depending on the story, these steps might take a producer from 10 minutes to an hour to edit, or longer for actively developing news. Now with such public LLM-based tools OpenAI's Chat GPT, Google Bard, Anthropic Claude, and many others, such tasks may be done in seconds.

Tech Shake-Up: Sam Altman Returns as OpenAI CEO Amidst Board Overhaul

By Matt O'Brien and Haleluya Hadero | Updated 3:30 PM EST, November 22, 2023

In a rapid and unexpected twist, Sam Altman, co-founder of OpenAI and the ousted leader responsible for ChatGPT, is making a comeback as CEO just days after his sudden firing. The swift reversal marks the end of a brief but intense power struggle that sent shockwaves through the tech industry, emphasizing the ongoing debates surrounding the safe development of artificial intelligence (AI).

OpenAI, headquartered in San Francisco, announced late Tuesday that an "agreement in principle" has been reached for Altman's return, but this time under a restructured board of directors. The resolution followed intense negotiations initiated on Saturday, revolving around disagreements over Altman's role and the revamped board's composition, as revealed by an unnamed source close to the discussions.

While an independent investigation into Altman's dismissal continues, focusing on concerns initially raised by the company regarding Altman's alleged lack of transparency, no serious wrongdoing has been identified. The source mentioned a gradual erosion of trust as the primary factor.

The opaque circumstances surrounding Altman's firing triggered internal conflicts and external pressures from investors, notably Microsoft. The tech giant swiftly hired Altman and OpenAI co-founder Greg Brockman, offering an open door to more than 700 OpenAI employees.

This power struggle brought to light deep-seated differences between Altman, a prominent figure in the rapid commercialization of generative AI, and board members expressing reservations about the safety risks associated with advanced AI.

The majority of the previous board resigned, making way for a new board led by former Salesforce co-CEO Bret Taylor, with former U.S. Treasury Secretary Larry Summers and Quora CEO Adam D'Angelo joining. The only member retained from the prior board is Adam D'Angelo.

Johann Laux, an expert at the Oxford Internet Institute, commented, "The OpenAI episode shows how fragile the AI ecosystem is right now, including addressing AI's risks."

Microsoft, a major investor in OpenAI, played a crucial role in facilitating Altman's return, underlining the importance of resolving governance issues. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella expressed optimism about the changes, considering them an essential step toward more stable and effective governance.

The new board structure raises questions about potential changes, given OpenAI's transformation from a nonprofit with a mission to build safe AI benefiting humanity to a for-profit business with a nonprofit board of directors. Past executive turmoil, including a 2018 fallout between Altman and Elon Musk, reflects the organization's history of leadership challenges.

The new board will collaborate on potential structural changes, considering that the current model mandates profits beyond a certain cap to be reinvested in the mission. OpenAI is also tasked with determining when AI surpasses human capabilities in economically valuable work.

Microsoft's commitment to OpenAI's success extends to Greg Brockman, who will play a key role in ensuring the organization continues to thrive. Interim CEO Emmett Shear expressed satisfaction with the resolution, marking the end of a tumultuous 72 hours.
